What is Affiliate Marketing?

Affiliate marketing is the process of earning a commission by promoting other brands or people’s products. The affiliate simply searches for a product they enjoy, then promotes that product and earns a piece of the profit from each sale they make. The sales are tracked via affiliate links from one website to another.

Where do you fit into the picture?

Affiliate marketing at it’s the very core is about relationships, a relation between three parties i.e., Publishers, The affiliate or advertiser & The consumer.

Let’s delve into the complex relationship these three parties share to ensure affiliate marketing is a success.

Publishers

Also known as the creator, the seller, the brand, the retailer, or the vendor. This is the party that creates the product. They engage consumers through a variety of distribution models including websites, social media, search, email, mobile apps, offline marketing and more.

The Affiliate or Advertiser

An affiliate or advertiser promotes one or multiple affiliate products experiences, and services and tries to attract and convince potential customers of the value of the merchant’s product. If the consumer does end up buying the product, the affiliate receives a portion of the revenue made.

The Consumer

Whether the consumer knows that they are part of an affiliate marketing system or not, they are the drivers of cohort marketing. Affiliates share these products with them on social media, blogs, and websites. When consumers buy the product, the seller and the affiliate share the profits.

Sometimes the affiliate will choose to be upfront with the consumer by disclosing that they are receiving commission for the sales they make & sometimes they don’t.
